About Rice:
Boasting a 300-acre, tree-lined campus in Houston, Texas, Rice University is ranked among the nation’s top 20 universities by U.S. News & World Report. Rice has a 6-to-1 undergraduate student-to-faculty ratio and a residential college system that supports students intellectually, emotionally, and culturally through social events, intramural sports, student plays, lecture series, courses, and student government. Developing close-knit, diverse college communities is a strong campus tradition, which is why Rice is highly ranked for best quality of life and best value among private universities.

Rice is also a wonderful place to work. Rice faculty, staff, and students share values that are essential to our success as a healthy community. These values guide our decisions and behaviors and shape Rice’s culture. They come through in the way we treat each other and the welcome we extend to our visitors. These values can be recalled simply by our name — RICE — Responsibility, Integrity, Community, and Excellence.

Position Summary:
Reporting to the Chief Information Officer (CIO) and VP, Technology Solutions & Services (TS&S), the Director, TS&S Business Operations & Governance is responsible for ensuring the CIO organization operates as a high-reliability, precision-managed function. This role oversees the operational, financial, compliance, and governance functions necessary for the effective execution of the CIO’s vision. The Director provides operational discipline, performance tracking, and risk oversight that enable the CIO and Deputy CIO to focus on strategy, innovation, and service delivery.

Ideal Candidate Statement:
The ideal candidate will be an operational leader with strong governance acumen, capable of managing complex financial, compliance, and process portfolios. They must possess the ability to collaborate across finance, HR, legal, and technology functions and bring a mindset of continuous improvement to the CIO organization.

On-campus position: This position is exclusively on-site, necessitating all duties to be performed in person. Per Rice policy 440, work arrangements may be subject to change.
