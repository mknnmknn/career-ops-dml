### "In My Rush to be Right, I Forgot to be Useful" — January 20, 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/my-rush-right-i-forgot-useful-daniel-levine-ndzgc/

Key positions:
- Professional touchstone: *"If I cannot articulate, as fully as possible, why something is the way it is, I am not yet in a good position to propose a solution."*
- Dominant failure mode in technology transformation: skipping context — understanding why the current system exists — in favor of speed
- Context defined as: business drivers, multiple stakeholder levels, interrelationships and dependencies
- Equally dangerous for external consultants (speed-incentivized) and internal leaders (tempted to steamroll)

**Proof point value:** Articulates a distinct, hard-won philosophy of technology leadership. Differentiates from executives who lead with "fix it fast."

### "Working With Claude and CoPilot" — (~March 2026, continuation of Vibes series)

Key positions:
- Direct hands-on experience: WordPress, small automations, incremental refactors with Claude and CoPilot
- LLM as "the most over-eager, enthusiastic junior intern who has memorized ALL THE DOCUMENTATION"
- Core value: removes friction of surrounding context knowledge so you stay focused on the goal
- Critical limitation: inconsistent output, dangerous agreeableness — "will agree with your input immediately and enthusiastically, regardless of quality"
- The feedback loop: use AI to educate yourself on best practices AND to correct the AI's behavior
- "You don't need to know exactly how to implement — but you need the analytical skills to ask the right questions"

**Proof point value:** Practical AI experience with specific failure modes and honest assessment. Destroys "executive who only talks about AI" perception.

### "Beyond Competence: Using AI as Its Own Mirror" — April 7, 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/beyond-competence-using-ai-its-own-mirror-daniel-levine-bgf1c/

Key positions:
- Most underexplored leverage: **turning the same AI that generated a problem into the tool for diagnosing and correcting it**
- Key organizational insight: *"Technical resources now DIRECT as much as — if not more than — they IMPLEMENT."* Seismic skill shift organizations are failing to account for in talent assessment
- Documentation for humans vs. documentation for AI are very different — deliberately structured two documentation streams
- Central conclusion: **"The primary danger is no longer that the tool will make mistakes — it's that you'll accept them."**
- Reflexive review principle applies to lightweight everyday AI usage, not just sophisticated agent frameworks

**Proof point value:** Most sophisticated piece in the series. The "you'll accept them" insight is board-level — reframes AI risk from technical failure to human governance failure.
